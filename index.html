Visualization for the bushfire in Australia happend from (around) October 1 2019 to Janurary 31 2020


Some context for this visualization to the general audience

This visualization work aims to provide you an understanding on the bushfire situation in AU during the period captioned.

Before looking into the visualization, there are a few points to clarify in order to prepare you with a more solid context.

About data source

  1. Data is collected by the satellite (MODIS 6) from NASA. This is a project called FIRE INFORMATION FOR RESOURCE MANAGEMENT SYSTEM (FIRMS) of NASA.
  2. You can download data from here.

About data attributes

  1. Brightness: According to NASA, the Brightness is referred to as brightness temperature: Channel 31 brightness temperature of the fire pixel measured in Kelvin.
  2. Day/Night time: This indicates the time when the satellite collects the data as there are different algorithms for detecting the temperature anomalies.
  3. Note that in our visualization interface, we can manipulate whether display the data by day or night. But in comparision view, both day and night time data will be counted.
  4. Confidence: It indicates the confidence of identifying the anomaly as a burning point. We adpoted 60% as a general treatment according to NASA.
  5. More info on data attributes can be found in here.
